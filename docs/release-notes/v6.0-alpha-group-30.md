# LifeOS v6.0.0-alpha.4 — Screenshot Group 30

Group 30 adds automation hardening, persisted observability and emergency controls without unattended execution.

## Delivered

- Persisted, provider-neutral automation health derived from rules, proposals, executions, occurrences, runs, step runs and incidents.
- A distinct fail-closed Emergency Stop that blocks Group 28 execution and Group 29 orchestration boundaries.
- Explicit Emergency Stop reset that leaves guarded execution paused and never resumes proposals or runs.
- Scoped sanitized incidents with exact affected state, last-safe checkpoint and explicit recovery options.
- Explicit retry with dependency revalidation and no automatic retry loop.
- Visible blocked-retry and blocked-preview reasons retained in audit.
- Reverse-order rollback preview with exact restore summaries and retained rollback history.
- Sanitized diagnostics with no secrets, connector payloads or private local paths.
- Eight approved fictional-data screenshots under `docs/screenshot-groups/group-30-automation-hardening/`.
- Repository hygiene validation required before commit and before final push.

## Manual-verification correction

Screenshot verification found that a failed step could be returned to Pending after its required dependency had already been rolled back. Pack 2 rejects that retry before mutation, leaves the run in `RecoveryRequired`, preserves the failed step and retry count, and displays the rejection reason visibly.

No background workers, schedulers, services, automatic continuation, automatic retry, external writes, scripts, processes, plugins or AI decisions were introduced.
